# CRA Construction — Brand System

> Single source of truth for all CRA content: website copy, Instagram captions, marketing materials.
> Both the website and the marketing agent pull from these rules.

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Confident, warm, builder-proud** — like a craftsman showing off work they genuinely love
- **Specific over generic** — name the material, the feature, the feeling it creates
- **First-person plural** — "We", "Our team", never corporate third-person
- **Arkansas-authentic** — grounded, no pretension, but clearly premium quality
- **Show, don't tell** — never use: amazing, stunning, incredible, beautiful, exceptional

## Headline Style

- Bold, short, declarative
- No punctuation in headlines unless it adds punch
- Examples that work:
  - "Built to Last"
  - "Every Detail Earned"
  - "Custom from the Ground Up"
  - "Your Build. Our Standard."

## CTA Language

- "Start your build →"
- "DM us to start your build →"
- "Tell us about your project →"
- "Talk with CRA"
- "View Our Work"
- Never: "Learn More", "Click Here", "Contact Us Today"

## Caption Structure (Instagram)

- **Format A:** One strong sentence. Under 15 words.
- **Format B:** Two short sentences. First sets the scene. Second lands the point.
- Max 20 words in the body
- 4-5 hashtags on their own line
- No sign-off line
- Max 1 emoji, end of caption only

## Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Paper | `#fffaf2` | Page background |
| Ink | `#191714` | Body text |
| Bronze | `#9a7041` | Accents, links, CTAs |
| Stone | `#f4f1ec` | Section backgrounds |
| Muted | `#756f66` | Secondary text |

## Typography

- Headings: serif (Cormorant Garamond or system serif)
- Body: system-ui / sans-serif
- All-caps with wide letter-spacing for labels and nav items

## What to Avoid

- Generic stock photo contractor template feel
- Too many icons/badges/cards
- Fake stats or overclaiming
- Busy hero sections
- Blog sections
- House plans / home plans sections
- Foundation sections
- Weak CTAs
- Long paragraphs above the fold
